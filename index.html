About the disease

Coronavirus Disease Outbreak Situation

COVID-19, also known as the coronavirus disease, is a highly contagious respiratory illness caused by the SARS-CoV-2 virus. It was first identified in Wuhan, China, in December 2019 and quickly spread globally, leading the World Health Organization (WHO) to declare it a pandemic in March 2020. COVID-19 primarily spreads through respiratory droplets when an infected person talks, coughs, or sneezes. The symptoms of COVID-19 can range from mild to severe, and some infected people may not display any symptoms at all. However, for some, it can lead to severe respiratory illness and even death. While vaccines and treatments are available, it is crucial to take preventive measures such as wearing masks, washing hands, and maintaining social distance to curb the spread of the disease.

  • Avoid touching your face.
  • Cover your mouth and nose when coughing.
  • Stay home if you feel unwell.
How to Protect Yourself?

Prevention & advice

Protecting yourself from COVID-19 is essential to prevent the spread of the virus. You can do this by following some simple measures, such as wearing a mask that covers your nose and mouth, washing your hands frequently with soap and water for at least 20 seconds, or using an alcohol-based hand sanitizer. Additionally, avoid touching your face, especially your eyes, nose, and mouth, and maintain at least six feet of physical distance from others when in public. It's also recommended to avoid crowded areas and gatherings, especially in poorly ventilated spaces. By taking these measures, you can protect not only yourself but also those around you from getting infected. Remember, we're all in this together, and each one of us has a role to play in preventing the spread of COVID-19.
